LogBrew gives you precise control over when your phone buzzes. You can filter by severity, mute alerts during off hours, and tune the app badge, all from **Settings, Notifications**. Your preferences apply across every project you monitor, so you configure them once and they take effect everywhere.

## Global notification filter

The notification filter determines which severity levels trigger a push alert.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings, Notifications">
    Tap **Settings**, then tap **Notifications**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a filter level">
    Select either **Critical only** or **All**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Critical only">
    Only events with **Critical** severity send a push notification. Warning and Info events are ingested and visible in your feed, but they do not trigger an alert or sound.

    This is the recommended default for most developers. Payment failures, crashes, and SLA breaches are critical. Deployment successes and info-level logs are not.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="All">
    Events with **Critical**, **Warning**, and **Info** severity all send push notifications. Use this when you need full visibility, such as during an active incident or when monitoring a new integration for the first time.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
  Switch to **Critical only** during focused work hours and use quiet hours (below) to silence nights and weekends. This combination keeps you informed about real emergencies while eliminating routine noise.
</Tip>

## Quiet hours

During quiet hours, push notifications are suppressed. No alert sound, no banner, and no lock-screen interruption. Your live feed continues to update normally in the background, so you can review everything that happened when you open the app.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings, Notifications, Quiet Hours">
    Tap **Settings**, tap **Notifications**, then tap **Quiet Hours**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your start and end times">
    Choose the time you want alerts to stop (start) and the time you want them to resume (end). For example: start at 10:00 PM, end at 8:00 AM.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your timezone">
    Pick the timezone that matches your schedule. LogBrew applies quiet hours relative to the timezone you choose, not the device's current timezone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable quiet hours">
    Toggle quiet hours on. The schedule takes effect immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Events that arrive during quiet hours are still saved to your feed. When quiet hours end, any unread events are waiting for you in the Feed tab exactly as they arrived.
</Note>

## App badge

The red badge on the LogBrew icon shows a count of unresolved events. You can choose what that count represents.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings, Notifications, Badge">
    Tap **Settings**, tap **Notifications**, then tap **Badge**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a badge mode">
    Select **Critical count** or **All unresolved**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Critical count (default)">
    The badge shows the number of unresolved **Critical** events across all your projects. Resolving a critical event reduces the count immediately.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="All unresolved">
    The badge shows the total number of unresolved events across all severities: Critical, Warning, and Info. Use this if you want the badge to reflect your complete backlog.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Starred log lines

You can star individual log lines in the Logs tab to flag them for follow-up. LogBrew can also send you a push notification whenever a new log line matching a starred pattern arrives.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings, Notifications">
    Tap **Settings**, then tap **Notifications**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable Starred logs">
    Toggle on **Starred logs**. From that point forward, log lines that match your starred patterns trigger a push alert, subject to your active filter level and quiet hours schedule.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Notification preferences apply globally across all your projects. There is no per-project override for severity filter, quiet hours, or badge mode.
</Note>
